Privacy Policy

Transparency and data protection for Jira Helper

🛡️ 1. Introduction

Welcome to the Privacy Policy for Jira Helper – Zephyr Scale Filler ("we," "our," or "the extension"). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have complete visibility over how the extension handles your data.

Our core philosophy is simple: Your data stays on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or sell any personal information or test data.

📦 2. Data Collection & Processing

Jira Helper is designed to operate entirely locally within your browser. We adhere to strict data minimization principles:

  • No Telemetry or Tracking: We do not track your usage, behavior, or interactions with the extension.
  • No External Servers: The extension does not communicate with any external servers, databases, or third-party APIs (other than the Jira APIs invoked directly from your authenticated Jira tab).
  • Local Test Data: Any test scripts, step descriptions, or expected results you paste into the extension are processed locally in your browser memory and are never sent to us.

🔑 3. Explanation of Permissions

To provide its automated auto-fill functionality, Jira Helper requires the following specific Chrome extension permissions:

  • Host Permissions (*://*.atlassian.net/*): Required strictly to interact with the DOM of your Jira Zephyr Scale test cases. This allows the extension to automatically type your test steps into the inputs and trigger "Add Step" events on your behalf.
  • activeTab & webNavigation: Used to detect when you are actively on a Zephyr Scale page and to gracefully handle page refreshes or iframe loading.
  • sidePanel: Allows the extension to open its user interface in a convenient, persistent side panel while you work in Jira.
  • storage: Used exclusively to save your local extension preferences (like custom input delays). This data is stored locally in your browser and is never synced to external servers.

🔒 4. Security & Compliance

Because the extension operates solely on the client side, the security of your test data is governed by your own browser and machine security. It leverages your existing Jira authentication session and cannot bypass any Atlassian security protocols.

🔄 5. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. Any changes will be posted on this page.
